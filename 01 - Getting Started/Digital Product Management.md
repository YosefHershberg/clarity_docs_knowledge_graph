# Getting Started with Digital Product Management

## Overview

Digital Product Management (DPM) in Clarity shifts the organizational mindset from managing individual projects to managing products and value streams.

## Key Principles

1. **Product-Centric Thinking** — Organize work around products and services rather than temporary projects
2. **Persistent Teams** — Fund teams, not projects. Teams are organized around products and value streams
3. **Continuous Delivery** — Move from project milestones to continuous value delivery
4. **Strategic Alignment** — Use [[_MOC Roadmaps|Roadmaps]] and [[Working with Objectives|Objectives]] to connect work to strategy

## How Clarity Supports DPM

### Strategic Layer
- **[[Working with Objectives|Objectives]]** — Define organizational OKRs
- **[[_MOC Roadmaps|Roadmaps]]** — Visualize how investments align with strategy
- **[[_MOC Hierarchies|Hierarchies]]** — Define parent-child relationships between investments

### Execution Layer
- **[[_MOC Projects|Projects]]** — Manage complex initiatives with tasks, milestones, and phases
- **[[_MOC Investments|Custom Investments]]** — Flexible investment types matching your terminology
- **Teams** — Persistent teams funded around value streams

### Management Layer
- **[[_MOC Resources|Staffing]]** — Allocate people based on skill, geography, and availability
- **[[_MOC Financials|Financials]]** — Track planned, actual, and forecasted budgets
- **[[_MOC Timesheets|Timesheets]]** — Capture actual time spent on investments
